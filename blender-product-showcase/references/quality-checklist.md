# Blender product showcase quality checklist

## Geometry and hierarchy

- Product dimensions match the specification.
- Visible parts use meaningful English names.
- Assembly parents and origins are correct.
- Animated components rotate around real pivots.
- Applied scale and normals are consistent.
- No visible intersections, z-fighting, gaps, or floating parts.
- Bevel width matches the product scale.
- Small repeated details are instanced when practical.

## Materials

- Metal has continuous controlled highlights and does not look like gray plastic.
- Plastic and rubber remain distinguishable without crushing to black.
- Glass retains reflections without hiding display content.
- Texture scale, UV orientation, and color space are correct.
- Emission retains color detail and does not clip.
- Micro-surface effects remain subtle at normal viewing distance.

## Studio and lighting

- Background fills the complete aspect ratio without seams.
- Product silhouette remains readable in dark shots.
- Key light explains form; rim lights separate the subject.
- Warm and cool accents do not overpower neutral materials.
- Lights and reflection cards are not visible unintentionally.
- Contact shadow and floor reflection communicate height and weight.

## Cameras and animation

- Product scale in frame matches the brief.
- No required part is cropped in approval shots.
- Focal length avoids unintended wide-angle distortion.
- Motion uses deliberate easing and coherent screen direction.
- Focus targets and depth of field remain stable.
- Cuts occur on intentional action or visual continuity.
- Final hold is long enough for titles or editing.

## Brand and content safety

- No unauthorized logo, product name, boot mark, UI brand, or watermark appears.
- Reference images are not used as the final background unless requested.
- Supplied assets are used only for their declared purpose.

## Delivery

- `.blend` opens without missing paths.
- Stage scripts can be rerun without duplicating objects.
- Preview frames cover the whole timeline.
- Full rendered frame count matches the specification.
- MP4 resolution, duration, frame rate, codec, and pixel format are verified.
- Hero still has the requested resolution.
- The user can distinguish viewport playback from a true render.
